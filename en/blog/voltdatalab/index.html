Projects - Volt Data Lab

Lucas Gelape
Jul 23, 2020

As a data analyst at Volt Data Lab, I participated in some projects that involved data collection, analysis and visualization, from many different sources.

  • Science Pulse: online tool to connect journalists to scientific conversation in social media, by finding content from a list of trustworthy sources of experts/researchers who engage on social media (mainly on Twitter). This project involved working with R on remote servers and Shiny.

  • R Packages: two packages to extract data from APIs developed by Volt Data Lab: pollingBR, which gets data about electoral polls compiled by news website Poder360; and crossfire, that gets data about gun shootings in Rio de Janeiro and Recife’s metropolitan areas, collected by the Fogo Cruzado initiative.

  • Data and statics on podcasts in Brazil, 2019 (in Portuguese): report produced by Sérgio Spagnuolo and I, in which we collected and analysed data on Brazilian and International podcasts hosted on Pocket Casts. Data was collected in July and August, 2019 and the code can be found on GitHub.

  • Terra de Resistentes: collaborative project which investigates threats to environmental leaders and their communities in seven countries on Latin America. I contributed with data analysis and visualization on threats to indigenous communities in Rondônia. This story involved data analysis from indigenous lands, deforestation, illegal roads, hidrography, mining and rural lands.
